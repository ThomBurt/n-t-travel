A Long Weekend on the Amalfi Coast

Italy is a country that is on many travelersâ€™ wish list. And why not? Italy has it all: fabulous cuisine, gorgeous cities, towering mountains, beautiful beaches, and ancient historical sites.

The Amalfi Coast is one of Italy's gems and a very popular tourist destination. In this blog, we will be sharing our itinerary to see the very best of this stunning coastline in 4 days, the perfect long weekend break.

Day One: Naples, Pompeii & Sorrento

When you arrive in Naples, exit the airport and you will see many taxis and car rental vans waiting for you. We would advise only to rent a car if you are comfortable driving in southern Italy where the roads are narrow and the driving is more aggressive than what you might be used to.
Quick tip: If you choose to rent a car, book it ahead of time to save money! Also know that parking is difficult to find (and expensive).

Book ahead of time...

Private transfer to Amalfi Coast from Naples Airport with Pompeii stop
Pompeii tour with Archaeologist, SKIP THE LINE!

Driving from Naples Airport to the ruins at Pompeii is roughly a 20 minute drive. Once you arrive you will find a car park, or if you have decided to take the train or taxi it will drop you off right outside the ruins.
Pompeii is magnificent and definitely one you do not want to miss when visiting the Naples region.

A visit to Pompeii will take anywhere from 2-4 hours and is truly fascinating. We do recommend this tour as you will be taken around by an archaeologist who will share the history perfectly, and in a place like Pompeii you find yourself wanting to learn more and more...

Quick tip: The view of Mt Vesuvius from the Forum is magical... ask your tour guide to get a photo of your group here...

After Pompeii, continue your journey up into Sorrento, which is roughly a 45 minute drive. No matter where you decide to stay, Sorrento, Positano, Amalfi, or any other town. You will have an incredible time.
We do recommend Sorrento as it is the most central town with a train station and great ferry terminal. Ferries are the best way to see the Amalfi Coast!

Finish the evening off with a lovely dinner by the ocean, you can see Naples and Mt Vesuvius across the bay, and there is no better place in the world to enjoy a bottle of wine and a pizza!

Day Two: Sorrento

Sorrento is a great town on the Amalfi Coast, you can get a train to Pompeii or Naples, it has numerous bus stops and a great ferry terminal, when you're on the coastline the ferry is the only way to go. Easily get the ferry to Capri, Positano, Amalfi, Salerno, and more...

The markets and shops in Sorrento are one not to miss, and we definitely recommend stopping for lunch at A'Marenna a beautiful little sandwich shop owned by two sisters. The food was incredible, and be sure to take your sandwiches one minute down the street to enjoy it on a bench over looking Naples and Mt Vesuvius across the Bay of Naples. You can't beat it!

Spend some time at the beach and perhaps take a nap to the sound of the waves, and take some rest. Get too hot? Not a problem, take a dip in the ocean to cool off!

Dinner

There is a wealth of options to choose from for dinner in Sorrento, and we would recommend Bellevue Syrene as the food is beautiful and it overlooks the water. Enjoy watching the sunset with a pizza and bottle of wine, and perhaps a cheeky glass of Limoncello!
Has to be done right?

Day Three: Positano & Amalfi

From Sorrento, grab ferry tickets and catch the 40 minute ferry ride to the beautiful and world famous town of Positano.
Positano is one of those bucket list places to visit, the ferry ride over is stunning and once you arrive you are greeted by artists, musicians, the beach and a great row of restaurants, perfect for breakfast, brunch or lunch (depending what time you arrive).

Ferries run very couple of hours, but in the busy season especially we advise booking ferry tickets ahead of time on a site like Ferry Hopper.

In Positano, browse the many shops, relax at the beach, walk the beautiful streets and definitely grab a glass of wine or Limoncello, you won't regret it!

After you have spent some time in Positano, hop on a 15 minute ferry to Amalfi, which is our favorite place on the Amalfi Coast. Enjoy the restaurants, beach, live music and local bars. The last ferry back to Sorrento leave at 17:05, so we recommend either spending the night in Amalfi, or booking a private taxi back to Sorrento, or taking the below day trip.

If you feel pressed for time and do not want to negotiate the public transport, we recommend this day trip which will pick you up from your hotel and take you in an air-conditioned car to Positano, Amalfi and Ravello, all beautiful locations and a perfect day trip that is totally affordable.

Dinner

Whether you decide to stay in Positano or make the trip to Amalfi, there are numerous restaurants available to choose from, all with fantastic Italian food and a great wine and beer list.
